Services

How we help research teams.

Two complementary service lines, drawn from hands-on experience across the clinical trial lifecycle.

01

Clinical Research Associate Services

We provide independent and embedded clinical research associate support, monitoring trial sites both on-site and remotely to ensure studies are conducted in line with the protocol, ICH-GCP, and applicable regulatory requirements. The focus is always on data integrity and participant safety, in both blinded and unblinded capacities.

What this includes

  • Site qualification, initiation, routine monitoring, and close-out visits
  • Source data review and source data verification, applying an SDR-first methodology
  • Protocol and GCP compliance oversight
  • Investigational product accountability, including blinded and unblinded duties
  • Identification, reporting, and documentation of AEs and SAEs within required timelines
  • Investigator site file and TMF maintenance for continuous audit readiness
  • Query management, site coaching, and first-cycle monitoring visit reports
02

Consulting & Quality Systems

We work with sponsors, sites, and research organisations as a strategic partner, bringing experienced clinical research and systems judgement to the decisions that shape a study's success. Engagements can be one-off advisory work or ongoing support.

Areas of focus

  • Trial design and feasibility input
  • Vendor assessments and vendor management
  • SOP development, review, and implementation
  • Quality systems and process optimisation
  • Risk-based monitoring strategy
  • Audit and inspection preparation
  • CTMS and clinical systems advisory
03

Regulatory & Ethics Submissions

We prepare, compile, and manage the regulatory and ethics submissions that a clinical trial depends on, keeping applications accurate, complete, and on time so your study can start and stay open without avoidable delays.

Regulatory authorities

  • Clinical Trial Application (CTA) preparation and submission to SAHPRA
  • Submissions to other national and international regulatory bodies as required
  • Amendments, annual progress reports, and renewals
  • Safety reporting and management of regulatory queries
  • Import permits and associated regulatory documentation

Ethics committees

  • Submissions to Independent Ethics Committees and Institutional Review Boards (IEC/IRB)
  • Submissions to Pharma-Ethics and institutional research ethics committees
  • Initial applications, amendments, and continuing review submissions
  • Coordination of responses to ethics committee queries
04

Source Data Development

Good data starts at the point of capture. We design and refine source documents, worksheets, and site tools so that information is recorded accurately and consistently the first time, reducing queries and supporting cleaner database locks.

What this includes

  • Design of protocol-specific source documents and worksheets
  • Review and improvement of existing site source documentation
  • Development of trackers, visit checklists, and monitoring tools
  • Alignment of source documents with protocol and eCRF requirements
05

Training & GCP Certification

We train and mentor individuals entering clinical research, building the practical skills needed for CRA and site-based work. Training draws on real monitoring and systems experience rather than theory alone.

Individual training

  • Foundational and ongoing training for aspiring and junior CRAs
  • Site staff training on study conduct, documentation, and data quality
  • Mentoring on systems, processes, and therapeutic-area knowledge
